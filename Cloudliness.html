Comparison: Latitude vs. Cloudiness

A good explaination about the impact of Cloud cover was provided by the University of Arizona in their website

In general, clouds are a cooling influence during the day by reflecting solar radiation, and a warming influence at night because they emit infrared radiation. Locations with high amounts of cloud over tend to experience less temperature variation over a day (cooler days, warmer nights) compared with locations with clear conditions.

In the random data set there doesn't seem that there was any measurable differences in Cloudiness based on the latitude. The data plots seem to be equally distributed.
