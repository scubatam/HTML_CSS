Latitude vs. Wind Speed

Let's start off with the definintion of how is wind speed is defined. It is the movement of air in any direction. The speed varies from calm to the very high speeds of hurricanes. Wind is created when air moves from areas of high pressure toward areas where the air pressure is low.

There are four forces that influences wind speed and wind direction: Temperature, Air Pressure, Centripetal Acceleration and the Earth's Rotation.

In reviewing the plots on the the Scattplot, there is an equal distribution. This visual would make it a little difficult to be able to understand the overall impact of latitude on Wind Speed.

For a deeper dive on how latitude influences temperature, additional informations can be found on Temperature Visualization Plot page.

According to sciencing.com:

"Air temperature varies between day and night and from season to season due to changes in the heating Earth's atmosphere. Because of the sun's warming effect, there are more winds during the day. Air masses also differ in temperature. A warm front precedes a warm air mass. Warm air is less dense than cold air, so warm air rides up and over the cold air, causing winds. Conversely a cold front, the leading edge of a cold air mass, also creates wind."
