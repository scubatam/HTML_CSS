Comparison: Latitude vs. Temperature

Latitude is the most important factor that influences climate.

To help explain the values in the chart, the lower latitudes have significantly warmer temperature. These locations are nearer the equator and enjoy equally long days year long and the sun is just about directly overhead at midday.

Whereas, cities located the furthest from the equator (the higher latitudes) receive lower amounts of solar radiation. At the poles, the sun never rises very high in the sky. The sunlight is also filtered through a thick wedge of atmosphere, making the sunlight much less intense.
